A USA Today Network Special report

What we learned. How we did it.

A search for answers: Most of the border remains unfenced, ad more than half the existing fence blocks only cars, not people. But building a contiguous fence or wall is almost unimaginable. In Texas, it could require seizing as many as 5,000 parcels of private property. Border fences do inhibit crossings, but human smuggling and drug smuggling remain active once fences are built. There is no cost estimate for a border wall, and more crucially, there is no cost-benefit analysis: In some stretches, a wall might stop immigrants at a cost of more than $1 million per person.
